Claude-Ready Research Brief for Short Volatility
via Correlation and Dispersion Trades
Executive summary
This brief converts the idea “short volatility via correlation/dispersion trades” into a reproducible
research and implementation package: a measurement engine that detects correlation-structure
distortions (Δ correlation matrices, Frobenius distortion, eigenvalue/“market mode” concentration,
sector attribution, and correlation-of-correlations stability), a multi-layer signal stack (distortion →
tradable residual dislocation → calibrated mean reversion probability via OU/ADF/Hurst → regime
safety), and four deployable trade expressions (relative-value spreads, dispersion book, PCA residual
baskets, and synthetic short convexity). The package is grounded in primary references for (a) variance-
swap and volatility-index replication with option strips 1 , (b) implied correlation/dispersion
measurement and interpretation 2 , (c) correlation eigenstructure and “market mode” using random-
matrix/PCA perspectives 3 , and (d) rigorous backtest design and tail-risk reporting (including
Expected Shortfall) 4 .
Alpha thesis and trade expressions
Core alpha thesis (precise)
Let an equity index have constituents i = 1,…,N with weights w i, constituent volatilities σ i, and
pairwise correlations ρ ij. The index variance decomposes into idiosyncratic (single-name) variance plus
correlation-weighted cross terms (the same relationship used by implied-correlation calculations). 5
When the realized correlation structure deviates sharply from a stable baseline (a structure distortion),
and the deviation is not a true regime break, tradable residuals (spreads, factor-neutral baskets,
dispersion gaps, and implied–realized correlation wedges) often mean-revert. Dispersion P&L is
theoretically driven primarily by the implied vs realized correlation wedge, with additional higher-order
volatility (“volga”) terms that must be risk-managed. 6 Evidence that correlation risk is priced in
equity options supports the existence of persistent premia and systematic wedges exploitable by
correlation/dispersion strategies. 7
Notation (used throughout)
- Returns: r i,t = ln(P i,t /P i,t−1 )
- Return vector: r t ∈ R N
- Correlation matrix over window W: C t (W) ∈ [−1,1]N×N
- Index return: r I,t = ∑ i w i r i,t

Entry premise
Enter when (i) correlation structure distortion is high and (ii) the spread/residual is dislocated (large ∣z∣)
but statistically mean-reverting (OU/ADF/Hurst filters).
P&L driver (conceptual)
Profit comes from contraction of the spread (mean reversion), i.e., reduction in spread variance /
realized volatility of x t. Because the spread is hedged, the intended exposure is to residual dynamics
rather than directional market moves.
What this shorts
Primarily realized volatility of the spread (not necessarily option-implied volatility), and implicitly the
persistence of correlation distortions.
Trade expression B: Dispersion book (index vs components)
Dispersion is the canonical “short vol via correlation” expression, because index variance embeds
constituent variances and their correlations. 8

Implied-correlation indices operationalize a related relationship using implied vols/variances. 9
Trade construction (common form)
- Long dispersion: sell index straddle/variance, buy constituent (or top-constituent basket) straddles/
variance. 10
- Short dispersion (the user’s “short vol” flavor often maps here): buy index variance exposure and sell
constituent basket variance exposure, or more commonly, run a conditional short dispersion only when
correlation is expected to rise and idiosyncratic dispersion is expected to compress.
P&L driver
A key primary result: dispersion-trade P&L can be expressed as (i) a correlation P&L term plus (ii) a
volatility higher-order term (vega/volga/vanna effects). 6
Operationally, your “alpha” is the expected move in realized correlation relative to the implied or
“priced” correlation wedge (and whether you are positioned long vs short that wedge).
Important practical anchor
A standardized implied dispersion measure exists as an index: the Cboe S&P 500 Dispersion Index is
defined as a square-root of the difference between weighted constituent expected variance and
expected index variance (VIX²), floored at zero. 11 This provides a benchmark/feature for signals and
monitoring.

Trade expression D: Synthetic short convexity (controlled)
Goal
Create a position whose payoff is negatively convex to realized variance/correlation shocks—i.e., it
behaves like a short option gamma/short variance exposure but possibly constructed via liquid listed
instruments (options, variance proxies, or dynamic strategies).
Canonical option-theory anchor

using portfolios of options weighted by strike-dependent kernels; the Cboe volatility-index methodology

Controlled synthetic variants (examples for implementation, not recommendations)
- Delta-hedged short straddle (single-name or index) with explicit gamma/vega limits.
- Short variance exposure via listed variance proxies (if available) or via structured option strips
approximating variance exposure (subject to liquidity constraints).
- Dispersion-as-synthetic correlation short: a dispersion structure can generate near-pure correlation

This section is intentionally “audit-like”: it specifies how to produce a credible research result.
Universe selection
Two baseline universes (choose one; both supported): 1. Index + sectors: index proxy + sector ETFs/
futures for spreads/PCA (simpler, fewer survivorship issues).
2. Index + constituents: index constituents for dispersion and residual baskets (requires survivorship-

Universe rules (all explicit; values unspecified): - Liquidity filter (ADV threshold unspecified)

- Tradability constraints (options listing availability, min OI, etc.)
